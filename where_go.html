Community Baking

Where the Bread Goes

People at community baking eat some bread, and take some home for themselves and friends and family.

That currently accounts for only a small amount of the bread we bake, and we put rest on the shelves at the Worcester community fridges called the WooFridges.

Using the WooFridges to distribute our bread is a natural choice because, just like Community Baking, the WooFridges are oranized around principles of mutual aid, direct action, collectivism and anarchism.

Note that the decision of how to distribute our bread is not set in stone, and it can be changed at any time by general consensus.

About the WooFridges

The WooFridges are a mutual aid project that began during the COVID-19 pandemic.

There are 4 locations around the city of Worcester that have a fridge and shelving, right on the street. Their poicy is "Take what you need. Leave what you can.

Here is a link to their website: www.woofridge.org

And here is a link to a wonderful documentary about the WooFridges entitled "More Than a Fridge": More Than a Fridge It was produced by Alex O'Neil, and debuted in Worcester in 2024.
